Injera Recipe

Home

Description

Enjera is a soft, spongy sourdough flatbread, a staple in Ethiopian and Eritrean cuisine. With a tangy flavor and unique texture, it's both plate and utensil, perfect for scooping up stews and sauces.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour (or mix with all-purpose for milder taste)
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • Pinch of salt
  • (Optional: ¼ tsp instant yeast for quicker fermentation)

Steps

  1. Mix: Combine teff flour and water into a smooth batter. Cover and let ferment for 2–3 days until slightly bubbly and tangy.
  2. Stir & Salt: After fermentation, stir the batter and add a pinch of salt.
  3. Cook: Heat a nonstick or traditional clay griddle. Pour batter in a spiral motion, cover, and steam for 1–2 minutes. No flipping.
  4. Serve: Cool on a cloth. Serve warm with stews like doro wat or misir wot.
